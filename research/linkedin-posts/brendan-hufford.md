**Topic:** AEO — why SaaS companies fail in AI search  

> Traditional SEO is no longer enough. When buyers ask AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews for recommendations, most SaaS companies fail to appear.
>
> Why? Because they're optimized for search engines rather than answer engines. The gap is massive and most teams don't even know it exists.
>
> The fix isn't another keyword strategy. It's structuring your content to be "AI citation ready" — written in a way that LLMs can extract, trust, and cite.
>
> Stop chasing traffic. Start earning AI recommendations.

**Key Takeaway:** SaaS companies need to shift from optimizing for search engines to optimizing for answer engines (AEO). Content must be structured to be "AI citation ready" for tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity.

## Post 2

**Date:** 2026  
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/brendanhufford/recent-activity/  
**Topic:** Vanity metrics vs. revenue — SaaS content strategy  

> Stop obsessing over impressions and traffic.
>
> SaaS companies should focus on metrics that correlate with revenue, pipeline, and sales cycle reduction rather than chasing search volume or social media likes.
>
> The best content strategies I've seen in 2026 don't start with keyword research. They start with: "What questions do our prospects ask in the last 30 days before they buy?"
>
> Then you build Content IP — frameworks and stories that speak directly to your ICP, not generic "what is" blog posts that AI can write better than you.
>
> Link-building becomes link-earning when you create content worth citing.

**Key Takeaway:** Effective SaaS content strategy starts with buyer questions (not keyword research) and builds "Content IP" — unique frameworks that speak to the ICP and naturally earn links and AI citations.

**Topic:** Growth Sprints model — high-output SEO with lean teams  

> You don't need a 50-person content team to win at SEO.
>
> At Growth Sprints, we work with B2B SaaS companies ($10M-$100M ARR) using a sprint-based consulting model instead of traditional long-term retainers.
>
> The framework: identify the 20% of content that drives 80% of pipeline. Kill everything else. Then use AI as a first-draft machine, but always layer in human expertise, customer insights, and original data.
>
> The teams winning in 2026 aren't scaling content volume. They're scaling content quality and relevance with fewer, better pieces.

**Key Takeaway:** Lean SaaS teams can win at SEO by using sprint-based consulting models, focusing on the 20% of content that drives pipeline, and using AI as a first-draft tool enhanced with human expertise.
