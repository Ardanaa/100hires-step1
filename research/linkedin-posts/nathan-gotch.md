**Topic:** Bottom-up content framework for AI SEO  

> Traditional top-down funnel strategies are dying.
>
> The "bottom-up" content framework works better for AI search: start with what your customers actually ask, not what keyword tools tell you has volume.
>
> Ground your content in expert knowledge. Structure it to satisfy the specific queries AI models use to synthesize answers.
>
> Here's the system:
> 1. Interview your sales team — what questions do prospects ask before buying?
> 2. Map those questions to the reasoning journey AI takes
> 3. Create content that answers each step with unique expertise
> 4. Optimize for topical relevance and entity recognition, not keyword counts
>
> This is the framework we use at Rankability and teach in Gotch SEO Academy. It works for both traditional search and AI citations.

**Key Takeaway:** The "bottom-up" content framework starts with real customer questions (from sales teams) rather than keyword tools, mapping content to the AI reasoning journey for both traditional SEO and AI citation wins.

**Topic:** The 7-Step SEO campaign checklist for 2026  

> My 2026 SEO campaign checklist — 7 steps that actually move the needle:
>
> 1. Technical SEO audit (crawlability + AI crawler access)
> 2. Topical authority mapping (not just keywords — entity clusters)
> 3. Content gap analysis (what are AI models citing that you're NOT covering?)
> 4. AEO-optimized content creation (structured for AI retrieval)
> 5. On-page optimization (PageOptimizer Pro or Clearscope level depth)
> 6. Digital PR + brand mentions (the new backlinks)
> 7. Measurement framework (traditional rankings + AI citation tracking)
>
> The biggest mistake: skipping step 3. If you don't know what AI is citing in your space, you're flying blind.
>
> Full breakdown on my YouTube channel.

**Key Takeaway:** A modern SEO campaign must include AI crawler access auditing, entity-based topical mapping, AI content gap analysis, and brand mention strategies alongside traditional optimization.
