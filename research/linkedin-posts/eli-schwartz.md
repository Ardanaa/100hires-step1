## Post 1

**Date:** 2026  
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/schwartze/recent-activity/  
**Topic:** Stop chasing AI citations — visibility dashboards don't equal revenue  

> The industry is obsessed with AI visibility dashboards that count mentions or citations. These metrics are vanity metrics — they don't correlate with business outcomes, pipeline, or qualified revenue.
>
> It's the same mistake we made with rank tracking. We optimized for the metric instead of the outcome.
>
> AI answers are probabilistic. You can run the same prompt 5x and get different brand mentions each time. Building a strategy around citation counts is building on quicksand.
>
> Focus on what you can control: build a product worth recommending, and make it easy for AI to understand what you do and who you serve.
>
> Stop chasing AI citations. Start building products and content that are worth citing.

**Key Takeaway:** AI citation metrics are vanity metrics — probabilistic and disconnected from revenue. Product-Led SEO means building products worth recommending rather than gaming AI visibility dashboards.

**Topic:** Product quality beats SEO manipulation in the AI era  

> In an AI-driven search world, search engines are better at understanding intent and quality than ever before.
>
> Good products with basic SEO will outperform bad products with good marketing. Every time.
>
> The AI doesn't care about your backlink profile or your domain authority score. It cares about whether you're the best answer to the user's question.
>
> This is why Product-Led SEO matters more now than ever: embed your SEO strategy directly into your product roadmap. Don't bolt it on after launch.
>
> The companies winning in 2026 aren't the ones with the biggest content teams. They're the ones where the product IS the content.

**Key Takeaway:** AI search rewards product quality over SEO manipulation. Product-Led SEO means embedding SEO strategy into the product roadmap — the product itself becomes the best content.

## Post 3

**Topic:** Influence-based measurement — the right way to think about AI search  

> Rather than fixating on whether a brand is cited in an AI answer (which may not result in a click or a traceable conversion), we need "influence-based" measurement.
>
> How does being present in AI-generated responses influence subsequent searches? How does it affect overall brand purchasing decisions?
>
> The measurement framework for AI search should look more like brand marketing measurement than SEO measurement:
> - Brand lift studies
> - Assisted conversion tracking
> - Long-term sentiment analysis
> - Share of voice in AI responses
>
> The old SEO playbook of "rank → click → convert" is broken in AI search. The new playbook is "be present → influence perception → drive downstream behavior."

**Key Takeaway:** AI search measurement should use brand marketing frameworks (brand lift, assisted conversions, sentiment analysis) rather than traditional SEO metrics. The model shifts from rank-click-convert to presence-influence-behavior.
