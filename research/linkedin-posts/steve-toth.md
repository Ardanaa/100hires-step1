**Topic:** User Embedding Edge — niche authority for AI citations  

> Stop trying to rank for everything. Start owning your niche so deeply that LLMs have no choice but to cite you.
>
> I call this the "User Embedding Edge." Instead of broad content, niche your content so that AI models — ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity — identify your brand as THE definitive authority for your specific Ideal Customer Profile.
>
> When an AI model encounters a query in your niche, your brand should be so embedded in the training data and web presence that it becomes the default citation.
>

> - Create the most comprehensive content on your specific topic (not adjacent topics)
> - Get mentioned by the authoritative sources AI models trust
> - Build a "Brand Truth" framework — verifiable claims that AI can validate
>
> Generic content gets generic AI treatment. Niche authority gets citations.

**Key Takeaway:** "User Embedding Edge" means building such deep niche authority that AI models default to citing your brand. This requires comprehensive, verifiable content on specific topics rather than broad coverage.

**Topic:** Query Fan-Out Optimization — the reasoning journey  

> Stop targeting isolated keywords. Start optimizing for the "reasoning journey."
>
> When an AI search engine processes a query, it doesn't just look at one page. It follows a sequence of sub-queries — I call this "Query Fan-Out."
>
> Example: A user asks "best CRM for small agencies." The AI might internally query:
> 1. "What features do small agencies need in a CRM?"
> 2. "Which CRMs have agency-specific features?"
> 3. "CRM pricing for small teams"
> 4. "CRM user reviews from agency owners"
>
> If your content answers steps 1-3 but not step 4, you lose the citation to a competitor who covers the full journey.
>
> The fix: map the reasoning journey for your key queries. Create content that covers every sub-query the AI might explore. That's Query Fan-Out Optimization.

**Key Takeaway:** "Query Fan-Out Optimization" means mapping and covering the full sequence of sub-queries an AI processes when answering a question, not just targeting isolated keywords.

**Topic:** The 2026 "Kill List" — outdated SEO tactics to abandon  

> Here's my 2026 "Kill List" — tactics from 2025 that are now actively hurting you:
>
> ❌ Publishing 20+ generic blog posts per month (AI writes better generic content than you)
> ❌ Buying backlinks for domain authority (AI models don't care about DA)
> ❌ Optimizing for featured snippets only (AI Overviews have replaced many)
> ❌ Creating content for every keyword variation (entity optimization > keyword optimization)
> ❌ Ignoring brand signals (off-page reputation is now the #1 differentiator)
>
> What to do instead:
> ✅ Publish fewer, better pieces with original data
> ✅ Invest in brand mentions and co-citations
> ✅ Structure content in 100-300 token passages for AI retrieval
> ✅ Build topical authority through content clusters
> ✅ Focus on the reasoning journey, not individual queries
>
> The bar has changed. Raise your game or get replaced.

**Key Takeaway:** Steve's "Kill List" identifies outdated tactics (generic content scaling, link buying, keyword stuffing) and replaces them with AI-era priorities: original data, brand mentions, structured passages, and reasoning-journey optimization.
