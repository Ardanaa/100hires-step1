**Topic:** SEO agencies in the AI era — adapting vs. dying  

> The agencies that survive 2026 are the ones moving away from simple content scaling toward human-led strategies that use AI as a "copilot."
>
> Google's algorithm is essentially math. Despite the rise of AI search engines like Perplexity and Gemini, the fundamentals haven't changed — quality, relevance, trust.
>
> What HAS changed is the bar. AI can produce "good enough" content at scale. That means "good enough" is now the floor, not the ceiling. Agencies need to deliver insights, testing, and optimization that AI can't do alone.
>
> The value isn't in content production anymore. It's in content intelligence.

**Key Takeaway:** AI raises the floor for content quality, so agencies must shift from content production to content intelligence — delivering testing, insights, and optimization that AI can't replicate independently.
