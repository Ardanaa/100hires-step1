## Post 1

**Date:** June 18, 2026  
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_two-brands-can-run-identical-on-page-optimization-activity-7473336241837731841-Dl3f  
**Topic:** Off-property reputation and AI citations  

> Two brands can run identical on-page optimization and get cited by AI at completely different rates.
>
> The gap is off-property reputation. AI reuses the trust already attached to the sources it pulls from, and it favors entities it already recognizes on a topic. It doesn't form a fresh opinion about your brand on every query.
>
> Your owned blog feeds that, but it's one of the weaker inputs. The model has no reason to take your word for your own authority. The publications, analysts, and competitors that mention you give it something it can trust without asking you.
>
> That's why I treat on-page work as just one lever for AI citations. With optimization roughly equal, the brand that wins is the one AI already trusts through other sources.
>
> Before you tune another title tag, check which sources AI cites in your space and whether any of them mention you.
>
> I broke the citation gap down in the latest Growth Memo.

**Key Takeaway:** On-page SEO alone won't win AI citations — off-property reputation (third-party mentions from trusted publications) is the differentiator. AI models reuse existing trust rather than recalculating it per query.

>

## Post 3

**Date:** February 2026  
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_in-seo-we-obsess-over-where-we-rank-but-activity-7423732576139476992-sx3r  
**Topic:** AI Brand Archetypes — how AI perceives your brand  

> In SEO, we obsess over where we rank. But in the age of LLMs, the more critical question is who the AI thinks we are.
>
> I call this your AI Brand Archetype.
>
> It's the mental model the AI has constructed of your brand. Does it see you as a Tool? A Publisher? A Crisis? Or a Guru?
>
> To test this, I analyzed Shopify using data from the SE Ranking API. I wasn't looking for standard keyword rankings; I wanted to see the intents where Google's AI explicitly cites the brand.
>
> The Results: I expected the AI to categorize Shopify as a "Tool" (associated with terms like platform, pricing, website builder).
>
> The data tells a completely different story.
>
> When you categorize the topics where the AI cites Shopify, a new archetype emerges:
> ~43% Motivation & Quotes ("inspirational quotes," "positive captions")
> ~22% Entrepreneurship Skills ("business ideas," "founder mindset")
> ...and only ~14% Commercial Terms ("pricing," "plans")
>
> The Insight: The AI is 3x more likely to cite Shopify for lifestyle advice than for software pricing. The underlying reason is that Shopify performs very well for "motivational quote" topics.
>
> ⚠️ A Note on the Data Model: This analysis assumes that standard search volume is a proxy for "AI Prompt Volume." That is a big assumption. While directionally useful to understand "Share of Intent," it's not a perfect 1:1 map of chat behaviors. Treat this as a strategic signal, not an exact science.
>
> The Strategic Takeaway: If you want to win in AI Search, you can't just target keywords. You need to think about how your organic ranks might translate to how LLMs view your brand (AI Brand Archetype).

**Key Takeaway:** In the AI era, brands need to understand their "AI Brand Archetype" — how LLMs perceive and categorize them. Shopify's case shows AI cites brands for unexpected intents (lifestyle vs. software), so brand strategy must actively shape LLM perception.
